Customer Returns and Refunds Practical Activity Guide

The purpose of this guide is to ensure team member/s know how to safely process a refund for our customers. It is also an opportunity for team member/s to practise the key steps of processing a return with their line manager at the service desk/central checkout.

Prior to completing the tasks in this guide ensure the team member has successfully completed the Customer Returns and Refunds learning module on our Learning Hub.

The task activities can be done in groups of team members depending on the number of registers available at either the service desk or central checkout.

It should take approximately 15 minutes to complete. It is divided into three parts.

Part A | Completion of safety reminders checklist by the line manager with team member/s | 3 mins

Part B | A practical demonstration given by the line manager with the team member/s on how to process: a refund with a receipt, a refund without a receipt, an online return refund | 10 mins

Part C | Confirm with team member/s they can safely and successfully process a customer refund | 2 mins

Part A Safety Reminders Checklist by the line manager with team member/s

Go through the Safety Reminders Checklist below with the team member at the service desk/central checkout.

Safety Reminders checklist

Task step | Task description
1 | Ensure service desk area is clean and tidy and free from trip hazards
2 | Ensure return tubs are sorted correctly and maintained
3 | Ensure the RLO/Faulty returns location is kept tidy
4 | Ensure there is a location under the counter for refund slips that are signed by customers
5 | Count the money in the register at the start of your shift (spot balance)
6 | Ensure you are wearing your PT2 (for calling a manager if required)
7 | Ensure cut resistant gloves are nearby for items returned that are broken, like glass or sharps
8 | Explain to always greet the customer politely and explain that you are there to try and help them
9 | Explain, if a customer approaches the service desk/central checkout to return an item, and does not have a receipt, use the PT2 to check in with the customer greeter to ensure any customers who enter without a receipt are validated
10 | Explain if a customer appears threatening explain to always adhere to the 5D's when dealing with threatening customer situations
11 | Explain if an item returned has an electrical fault, you are not to test/plug in these electricals
12 | Explain if an item returned is broken glass or a sharp, you must wear your cut resistant gloves when handling these returned items

Part B Practical demonstration given by the line manager with the team member/s on how to process: a refund with a receipt, a refund without a receipt, an online return refund

Use the tasks steps below as a guide to demonstrate how to process a refund. The task steps can be done alongside the manager, or the manager can demonstrate the task steps first, then ask the team members to perform the same task steps on their own.

For this demonstration, use any item receipt on hand. Manager to demonstrate and complete the tasks below with the team member/s at the registers/central checkout.

Process a refund with a customer receipt (for a customer who changes their mind)

Task step | Task description
1. | Log into register by scanning your operator ID or enter your operator ID
2. | Scan customer receipt
3. | Select purchased item listed on the screen
4. | Select type of return e.g., Changed mind
5. | Select the reason for the return, then press submit
6. | Select Finish to start the refund
7. | Check customer receipt to see how item was originally paid for
8. | Select the option that says how they purchased the item originally e.g., select cash option
9. | Confirm cash amount, press ok
10. | The system prints a new receipt, plus a store receipt for the customer to sign and return to store team member for filing

Manager to demonstrate and complete the tasks below with the team member/s at the registers/central checkout.

Process a refund without a customer receipt (for a customer who changes their mind for a purchase in store over $10)

Task step | Task description
1. | Log into register by scanning your operator ID or enter your operator ID
2. | Select Refund/Repair option on screen
3. | Select option "purchased in store". The system takes you to the refund screen
4. | Scan barcode on item
5. | Select type of return e.g., Changed Mind
6. | Select the reason for the return, then press submit
7. | Confirm price of refund, select OK
8. | Select Total to finalise the refund
9. | Enter the customer's ID e.g., enter their license number, then enter their first name and surname
10. | Select the ID type e.g., driver's license/permit
11. | Select Done
12. | Select Exchange Voucher (explain never give cash back without a receipt always select eVoucher only)
13. | Select Exchange only option (explain always select this option if no receipt)
14. | System prints three receipts:
    1. eVoucher
    2. A copy of receipt for customer and authorised team member to sign (because the item is over $10)

Process an online return refund (for a customer who has changed their mind)

Task step | Task description
---|---
1. | Select Refund/Repair
2. | Select Purchased Online Refund
3. | Enter online order number (or scan barcode on item). The screen displays original transactions details. Explain purchased items are listed so you can see each item as a separate line item. A line item highlighted in red indicates the item has already been refunded and cannot be selected
4. | Select the item for refund
5. | Select type of refund i.e., Changed mind
6. | Select the reason for the return, then press submit
7. | Select finish
8. | The screen displays a message that the refund will go back into the customer's account within five business days
9. | Select ok
10. | The system prints a new receipt for the customer
