## Page 3

A great place to shop

For Kmart to be a great place to shop we must truly 'think customer'.
This means opening our doors to a world class store that delivers a great experience for our customers, any time they visit us.
Here, we will assist our customers where required, and put them at the heart of everything we do.

Your stage
Once a day, we cut the ribbon on a world class store...
• Every available item on show
• Correct, clear pricing on every item
• General merchandise pulled forward to the edge of every shelf and peg
• Store presentation to standard (windows, prescribed VM, samples, eye-line, layouts and mannequins), as per team app
• Hang-wear and footwear sized
• Power tables folded and sized
• Trolley's and baskets full
• Registers and CPV's operational
• High cleaning and house-keeping standards
• Returns completed

All day, every day
Our customer deserves a great place to shop any time they visit us...
• Nothing on the floor
• Maintain product availability
• Items in general merchandise can be found behind the correct shelf edge label
• Items in clothing on the correct arm
• Trolleys and baskets available
• Store remains free from spills and rubbish
• Power tables are shoppable, with sizes and styles easy to locate
• Returns kept to 2 rails, 3 roll cages

Love the customer
Let's put the customer at the heart of everything we do...
• Service with a smile
• Acknowledgement of any customer we pass
• Offer to take the customer to the product as an alternative to providing directions
• When the register queue starts to snake, open another checkout where available
• Proactive, service focused customer greeters and self service checkout team leaders
• All team members have completed the Kmart Way Training relevant to their role
• Every team member in uniform with a name badge

A Great Place to Shop - key roles & objectives

Returns Team Members:
• Consolidation of "waste" into a single role
• Place back returns from various store collections points
• Achieve greater efficiency through better sortation
• Reduce returns workload for Store Presentation team

Refresh Team Members:
• Maintain the store at a reasonable standard for our customers
• Place back returns they collect
• Hand over a store that is in better condition to the Store Presentation team

Presentation Team Members:
• Restore the store to a "cut the ribbon" standard
• Place back returns they collect
• Hand over the store to the sequence team in an improved state, supporting more efficient and effective filling.

Refresh / Duty Manager:
• Allocate teams based on the forecasted/rostered team members
• Prepare the necessary plans and equipment for the refresh, returns and presentation teams
• Engage the teams at the start of each shift
• Lead and support the team during their shifts
• Minimise movement waste of the refresh and presentation teams

Providing customer service, is an essential requirement for all of these roles

Refresh overview

Each refresh shift, the team member will:

1. Collect a refresh cage and attend the huddle with other refresh and/or returns team members
2. Be assigned an area of the store for refresh that day
3. Commence the first refresh lap, prioritising any safety concerns and picking up all misplaced items
4. Sort collected items by world as they are placed on their refresh cage
5. Place collected items back on show as they come to them (on their laps)
6. Be provided with priority areas to complete targeted presentation, on subsequent laps (not first lap)
7. Provide collected items from other refresh areas and any S&D items/empty packets to the refresh manager when visited.
8. Provide any customer assistance as required

Summary of refresh principles

When
• End of Sequence until Presentation commences

What
• Safety issues
• Pickup from floor
• Pick up products visibly in the wrong place
• Collect rubbish and condense SRPs

Execution standards

How
Planning & Preparation
• Determine required number of team members.
• Prepared Planner & Pace Tool – assign team members to Worlds / priority areas

Management
• Huddle delivered to team

• Team members complete an initial ~30 minutes pick up/safety lap of their area (moving in one direction) and then continue laps completing priority areas as directed by manager.
• Put products back in their right location as they come to them (avoiding motion waste)
• Products from other areas should be sorted in refresh cage and collected by the Refresh manager for redistribution. RLO to be collected and managed appropriately.
• End of shift, return equipment cages to designated area and meet for huddle.

Returns sortation
Apparel

• Single micro-sorted rail at both fitting rooms and service desk (or behind)

• Discs used to micro-sort categories, to suit your store

• Discs remain on until rail is full

• Discs are removed and full rail is taken to BOH until it can be processed to the shop floor, or sorted onto single rails of the same category

• Move full rail to BOH for collection by returns team member(s)

Effective returns sortation improves time per cage/rail by up to 25% (13 minutes to 9 minutes, average)

• Apparel basics and power table lines sorted into totes ready for returns team member(s)

Returns sortation
Central Checkouts

While Checkout configurations vary the principles of return sortation should still be applied

• Dedicated returns area/location may be determined based on:
   • Checkout configuration
   • Returns volume
   • Space availability

• Micro sort wherever possible to improve efficiency, categories selected to suit your store i.e. Home world / Kids world / Clothing world
