Fitting Room Attendant Best Practice

Standards, role requirements and responsibilities of the Fitting Room Attendant:

Rostering Standards
When rostering for the Fitting Room Attendant role, managers must ensure:

• Ensure the Fitting Room Attendant completes a warm-up as per the Warm-Up's Poster prior to their shift and regularly walks around the fitting room area to maintain stretching.
• When a Fitting Room Attendant is on a break, the replacement Fitting Room Attendant must have completed the minimum training standards listed below.

Minimum Training Standards
All Team Members who are rostered in the Fitting Room Attendant role must have completed the following training:

• Fitting Room module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
• Bag check module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
• Safe Work Practice module completed for Trolleys, working at heights, scanning equipment, needle and pricing guns, merchandising.
• Fitting Room Attendant to have read, understood and comply to the Managing Threatening Situations – 4Ds Poster

Equipment Standards
Every Fitting Room Attendant must have the following equipment:

• Anti-fatigue mat.
• Push 2 talk units (P2T).
• PDT device if available or an EC30.
• Kmart utility belt containing:
    o Pen
    o Customer service forms
    o LP incident reporting forms
• Coloured number tags.
• Rubbish bin.
• Spare plastic hangers.
• Keys to lock fitting rooms, if the Fitting Room attendant needs to leave the area unattended.
• Cleaning equipment (gloves, cleaning spray, wipes and tissues).
• Kimble tags and needle gun.
• Pricing gun.
• RFID printer (to reprint barcodes)
• Safety step & mirror (to check areas high up e.g. tops of large mirrors).

Setup Standards
• Spare plastic hangers of various sizes available to hang apparel returns
• Clothing rails should be cleared of all returns from the previous day
• Number tags
• Anti-fatigue mats positioned behind the fitting room counter
• Storage area cleared so they can be used to hold non apparel stock for customers when they enter a fitting room.

Fitting Room Attendant Best Practice

Role Requirements
• A Fitting Room Attendant must have positive body language, friendly, knowledgeable, assertive, and confident.
• Fitting Room to be positioned behind the counter at the entrance of the fitting rooms.
• All threatening situations must be reported via the Safety Solvsafety portal.
• Service standards to be adhered to at all times (refer to section below).
• Report any incident from loss prevention to a manager who can submit an incident report.

Customer Service Standards

Customers Entering the Fitting Room
• Welcomes each customer as they approach the fitting room counter.
• Checks the number of apparel items the customer would like to take into a fitting room, and provides the customer with a number tag to identify how many items have been taken in.
• Requests to take any non-apparel items from the customer and places into a holding area, which are returned to the customer when they exit the fitting rooms.
• Conduct bag checks (as per the conditions of entry to the store) on all bags of A4 size and larger, prams, trolleys, cartons etc. If Kmart items are found, offer to hold the items while the customer tries on the apparel items.

If at any time a customer asks if they can leave their trolley, bag or child/children with you:
• Look after bags / trolley, advise the customer that you are unable to take responsibility for the items if there is any loss, theft or damage to them.
• Look after child / children, advise the customer that you are unable to watch their child / children as you may be called away at any time and Kmart cannot take responsibility for the child / children. You can offer for the customer to use the disabled fitting room that has more space to fit in the children while they try the apparel items on.

In-Between Service
• Hang and fold apparel items so they are ready to place onto the shop floor.
• Check each fitting room regularly to ensure they are clean, tidy and free from apparel, hangers and packing left behind. If syringes are located in a fitting room, call a manager immediately to collect and dispose of safely.
• Check fitting rooms regularly for empty packets and place into empty packet bins.
• Damaged garments should be placed aside and given to the manager to either mark down as a Soiled and Damaged clearance item or written off and disposed of.
• Replace tags on garments when tags have fallen off or missing.

NZ Stores Only: If a customer approaches to collect a click and collect order and there isn't a second Team Member at the fitting room desk, the Fitting Room Attendant must request assistance to ensure the desk area is not left unattended to collect the order.

Customers Exiting the Fitting Room
• As customer exit the fitting rooms, greet them asking 'how did you go?' and check the number of apparel items in the customer hand, matches the coloured number tag.
• If a customer returns with less apparel items than the number tag provided, ask the customer if they can go back to the fitting room and bring the additional items back. Once the customer has left inform a manager.
• Offer to take any apparel items the customer doesn't want to purchase.
• Return any non-apparel items placed on hold back to the customer.
• Reports non-Kmart items (lost property) left behind in a change room to a manager.

If at any time any of the above Customer Service Standards creates a threatening situation or aggressive behaviour from the customer always adhere to the 5D's and threatening situations. Report the incident to a manager discreetly.

Fitting Room Attendant Best Practice

Tasks Not to be Allocated to a Fitting Room Attendant

Fitting Room Attendants must be put in place to complete their role requirements and customer service only. Under no circumstances are the following tasks to be allocated to Fitting Room Attendants:
• Apply merchandise protection equipment to products.
• Escorting customers to products and departments within the store.
• Click and collect pick up.

What Good Looks Like

Fitting rooms must always be presentable and ready for use to ensure every customer has a great experience. This is achieved by:

✓ Keeping each fitting room clean by using the cleaning equipment to ensure mirrors, benches, seats and floors are always clean ready for use.

✓ Ensuring all apparel left behind is folded or hung back onto the return rack.

✓ Spare hangers are collected and hung with the spare hangers at the fitting room bench area.

✓ All rubbish is collected and placed into a bin.

✓ Empty packets are collected and placed into empty packets bins.

✓ Lost property is recovered and reported to a manager.
