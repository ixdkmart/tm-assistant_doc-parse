Customer Greeter Best Practice Guide

Standards, role requirements and responsibilities of the Customer Greeter:

Rostering Standards

When rostering for the customer greeter role, managers must ensure:

• A customer greeter is rostered during all trading hours in a store.
• 2 customer greeters are rostered for core and peak trading periods.
• All customer greeters to be provide updates for:
    o Updates relating to product (new products, out of stock etc.)
    o Store layout changes.
    o Conduct a lap of the store, to visually identify any changes.
    o Managers one duty should make them selves known the customer greeter.
• Ensure the Customer Greeter completes a warm-up as per the Warm-Up's Poster prior to their shift and regularly walks around the customer greeter area to maintain stretching.
• When a customer greeter is on a break, the replacement customer greeter must have completed the minimum training standards listed below.

Minimum Training Standards

All Team Members who are rostered in the Customer Greeter role must have completed the following training:

• Customer Greeter and bag check module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
• Managing Threatening Situations. Kmart Way module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
• Visitor management module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
• Customer Greeter to have read, understood and comply to the:
    o No Apprehension Policy.
    o Threatening Situations 4Ds
    o Receipt Scanner Process Guide

Equipment Standards

Every customer greeter must have the following equipment:

• Blue vest.
• Push 2 talk units (P2T).
• PDT device (needed for receipt scanning)
• Duress watch (where applicable).
• Safety camera (where applicable).
• Kmart utility belt holding:
    o Checkseals
    o Pen
    o Customer service forms
    o LP incident reporting forms
    o Portable EAS retractable de-tagger

Customer Greeter Best Practice Guide

Role Requirements
• Customer Greeter must be friendly, knowledgeable, assertive, and confident, with positive body language.
• Customer greeter to be positioned (refer to diagram examples on page 3):
    o Option 1 - One Customer Greeter
        ▪ For low and medium traffic times.
        ▪ To stand to the left side where customers exit to check receipts and bags, and farewell customers, however, roam the area to greet customers as they enter.
    o Option 2 - Two Customer Greeters
        ▪ For high and peak traffic times.
        ▪ One Customer Greeter at the entry to greet customers as they arrive, however, roam the area to greet customers as they enter/exit.
        ▪ One Customer Greeter at the exit to check bags, receipts and farewell customers as they exit, however, roam the area to greet customers as they enter/exit.
• All threatening situations must be reported via the Safety Solvsafety portal.
• Service standards to be adhered to at all times (refer to section below).
• Report Profit Protection incidents to a manager who can submit an incident report.
• Ensure EAS gates are tested daily and report to a manager if not working.

Customer Service Standards

Entry | Exit

1. Welcome each customer as they enter the store, with a greeting and a smile. | 1. Farewell each customer asking if the found everything they were looking for.

2. Assist customers with enquiries e.g.
• Location of products
• Direct customers for refunds/exchanges to the Service Desk and alert the team via P2T. | 2. Request receipt to:
• Scan as per the receipt scan process.
• Bag checks to ensure total items on receipt match what is in their bag, trolley, pram, box etc.

3. Reports suspicious behaviour discreetly using the P2T whilst adhering to the Managing Threatening Situations - 4Ds Poster | 3. Reports suspicious behaviour discreetly using the P2T whilst adhering to the Managing Threatening Situations - 4Ds Poster

4. Manages visitors and contractors who arrive at the store:
• Informs the management team.
• Ensures they scan in attendance using the Verified QR code.
• Applies checkseal on Team Member/visitor/contractor goods. | 4. Manages visitors and contractors who exit the store:
• Informs the management team.
• Ensures they scan out attendance using the Verified QR code.
• Checks checkseal on Team Member/visitor/contractor goods.

5. Monitors trolley and basket availability (using the P2T to notify a manager if replenishment required). | 5. Asks for assistance to queue bust whilst checking receipts with receipt scanner.

If at any time any of the above Customer Service Standards creates a threatening situation or aggressive behaviour from the customer always adhere to the 5D's and threatening situations. Report the incident to a manager discreetly.

Tasks Not to be Allocated to Customer Greeters
The customer greeter role can be overwhelming if tasks outside what is listed above are allocated. It is importance tasks such as the below are not allocated to this team member at any time:
• Merchandising e.g. folding apparel.
• Applying merch protection to products.
• Click and collect pickup
• Price markdowns.
• Escort customers to other areas.
• Layby pickup
