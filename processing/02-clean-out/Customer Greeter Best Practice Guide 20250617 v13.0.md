# Customer Greeter Best Practice Guide

Standards, role requirements and responsibilities of the Customer Greeter.

## Rostering Standards

When rostering for the Customer Greeter role, managers must ensure the following.
A Customer Greeter is rostered during all trading hours in a store.
2 Customer Greeters are rostered for core and peak trading periods.
Customer Greeters provide updates relating to product, including new products and out of stock items.
Customer Greeters provide updates on store layout changes.
Customer Greeters conduct a lap of the store to visually identify any changes.
Managers on duty should make themselves known to the Customer Greeter.
Ensure the Customer Greeter completes a warm-up as per the Warm-Ups Poster prior to their shift.
Ensure the Customer Greeter regularly walks around the Customer Greeter area to maintain stretching.
When a Customer Greeter is on a break, the replacement Customer Greeter must have completed the minimum training standards listed below.

## Minimum Training Standards

All Team Members rostered in the Customer Greeter role must complete the following training.
Customer Greeter and bag check module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
Managing Threatening Situations - Kmart Way module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
Visitor management module completed in LMS prior to being rostered.
Customer Greeter has read, understood and complies with the No Apprehension Policy.
Customer Greeter has read, understood and complies with the Threatening Situations 4Ds.
Customer Greeter has read, understood and complies with the Receipt Scanner Process Guide.

## Equipment Standards

Every Customer Greeter must have the following equipment.
Blue vest.
Push to talk units (P2T).
PDT device (needed for receipt scanning).
Duress watch (where applicable).
Safety camera (where applicable).
Kmart utility belt holding the following.
Checkseals.
Pen.
Customer service forms.
LP incident reporting forms.
Portable EAS retractable de-tagger.

## Role Requirements

Customer Greeter must be friendly, knowledgeable, assertive, and confident, with positive body language.
All threatening situations must be reported via the Safety Solvsafety portal.
Service standards must be adhered to at all times.
Report Profit Protection incidents to a manager who can submit an incident report.
Ensure EAS gates are tested daily and report to a manager if not working.

## Customer Service Standards

Entry

1. Welcome each customer as they enter the store with a greeting and a smile.
2. Assist customers with enquiries such as product locations and direct customers for refunds or exchanges to the Service Desk, and alert the team via P2T.
3. Report suspicious behaviour discreetly using P2T whilst adhering to the Managing Threatening Situations - 4Ds Poster.
4. Manage visitors and contractors who arrive at the store by informing the management team, ensuring they scan in attendance using the Verified QR code, and applying checkseal on Team Member, visitor, or contractor goods.
5. Monitor trolley and basket availability and use P2T to notify a manager if replenishment is required.

Exit

1. Farewell each customer and ask if they found everything they were looking for.
2. Request the receipt to scan as per the receipt scan process and conduct bag checks to ensure the total items on the receipt match what is in their bag, trolley, pram, or box.
3. Report suspicious behaviour discreetly using P2T whilst adhering to the Managing Threatening Situations - 4Ds Poster.
4. Manage visitors and contractors who exit the store by informing the management team, ensuring they scan out attendance using the Verified QR code, and checking checkseal on Team Member, visitor, or contractor goods.
5. Ask for assistance to queue bust while checking receipts with the receipt scanner.

If any of the above Customer Service Standards creates a threatening situation or aggressive behaviour from the customer, always adhere to the 5Ds and threatening situations.
Report the incident to a manager discreetly.

## Tasks Not to be Allocated to Customer Greeters

The Customer Greeter role can be overwhelming if tasks outside what is listed above are allocated.
It is important that tasks such as the below are not allocated to this team member at any time.
Merchandising such as folding apparel.
Applying merch protection to products.
Click and Collect pickup.
Price markdowns.
Escort customers to other areas.
Layby pickup.

## Option 1 - One Customer Greeter

For low and medium traffic times.
One Customer Greeter stands to the left side where customers exit to check receipts and bags and to farewell customers, and roams the area to greet customers as they enter.

## Option 2 - Two Customer Greeters

For high and peak traffic times.
One Customer Greeter is at the entry to greet customers as they arrive and roams the area to greet customers as they enter or exit.
One Customer Greeter is at the exit to check bags and receipts and to farewell customers as they exit and roams the area to greet customers as they enter or exit.
