# Policy and Procedure Document

## Goals
One simple way of working for core processes.  
Ensure consistency from site to site on important matters.  
Create a model that is easily learned, replicated, and embedded.  
Improve safety, enhance the customer experience, increase sales, and lower costs.

## Rollout Philosophy
Processes are built by stores, for stores.  
Maximize value to the customer through relentless pursuit of zero waste.  
Commit to embedding the core standards and principles.  
Evolve processes around these standards to adapt to environments.  
Bank any savings identified.

## A Great Place to Shop
Kmart must truly "think customer" to be a great place to shop.  
Deliver a world-class store that provides a great experience whenever customers visit.  
Assist customers as needed.  
Put customers at the heart of all activities.

### Your Stage
Once a day, open the store with the following standards:  
Every available item on display.  
Correct, clear pricing on every item.  
General merchandise pulled forward to the edge of each shelf and peg.  
Store presentation to standard, including windows, prescribed visual merchandising, samples, eye line, layouts, and mannequins, as per team app.  
Hang-wear and footwear sized.  
Power tables folded and sized.  
Trolleys and baskets kept full.  
Registers and CPVs operational.  
High standards of cleaning and housekeeping maintained.  
Returns completed.

### All day, every day
Customers deserve a great shopping environment at all times.  
Ensure nothing is on the floor.  
Maintain product availability.  
Items are correctly labeled behind shelf edges in general merchandise.  
Clothing items are on the correct arm.  
Trolleys and baskets are available.  
The store is free of spills and rubbish.  
Power tables are shoppable, with sizes and styles easy to locate.  
Returns are kept to 2 rails and 3 roll cages.

### Love the Customer
Put the customer at the center of all activities.  
Service with a smile.  
Acknowledge every customer passing by.  
Offer to escort customers to products instead of just giving directions.  
Open additional checkouts when queues form.  
Have proactive, service-focused greeters and self-service team leaders.  
All team members complete the relevant Kmart Way Training.  
Team members wear uniforms with name badges.

## Key Roles & Objectives

### Returns Team Members
Consolidate waste into a single role.  
Place back returns from various store collection points.  
Increase efficiency through better sortation.  
Reduce workload for the Store Presentation team.

### Refresh Team Members
Maintain store standards for customers.  
Place back returns collected.  
Hand over a store in better condition to the Store Presentation team.

### Presentation Team Members
Restore the store to a "cut the ribbon" standard.  
Place back returns collected.  
Support the sequence team with efficient filling.

### Refresh / Duty Manager
Allocate teams based on forecasted/rostered staff.  
Prepare plans and equipment for refresh, returns, and presentation teams.  
Engage teams at the start of each shift.  
Lead and support during shifts.  
Minimize movement waste for refresh and presentation teams.

Providing customer service is a critical requirement for all these roles.

## Store Operation Segments

The store operates in a circular flow:

Presentation: Prepare to "cut the ribbon" on a world-class store.  
Sequence: Stock items for customers to see.  
Shopkeeping: Maintain layouts and visual merchandising for easy filling and shopping.  
Refresh Returns: Keep the store tidy all day, every day.

Customer Service is at the center of these segments.

## Roles and Responsibilities by Time (Example: 7-22 hours)
- Refresh Manager: Lead refresh and returns teams, monitor progress, transfer stock.
- Duty/Extended Trade Manager: Lead store presentation, check quality, transfer stock.
- Returns Team: Process returns in shifts, manage volume.
- Refresh Team: Refresh store areas in shifts.
- Presentation Team: Present store in designated hours.
- Other Managers: Support and coach teams as needed.

Teams start and finish together.  
All teams participate in a team huddle.  
Refresh manager roles are less intense than sequence manager.  
Number of team members depends on daily forecast.  
All store areas are covered by refresh, returns, and presentation teams.

## Shop Keeping Overview
Shop keepers maintain layouts supporting sequence and presentation.  
They need structure, accountability, and engagement.  
They often do tasks that support others or address slack points.  
Identified as key opportunity during health checks, with notes, detailed input, and engagement.

## Shop Keeping Tasks
Address safety issues (empty arms/ends, missing shelf risers).  
Ensure pricing and ticketing compliance.  
Install new lines, adjust layout, fill end caps, and address poor performing lines.  
Make minor layout adjustments and close clearance gaps.  
Handle end-of-range markdowns.  
Fix visual merchandise displays and adjust internal windows.  
Ensure labels and clearance shelf talkers are in place.  
Filling priority backfills outside of sequence.

## Shop Keeping Notes
Use an equipment checklist: dress standards, phone, PDT, P2T.  
Communications include safety notice, loss prevention, and store message.  
Record tasks and notes with location, priority (HIGH/MED/LOW), and validation checkboxes.  
Notes are based on morning laps and team engagement.  
Managers validate completion.

## Shop Keeping Principles and Equipment
Shop keeping occurs at the end of sequence or parallel.  
Focus on safety, pricing, new lines, end changes, layout, end of range, VM, and labels.  
Use the Store Task Tool to create notes.  
Engage with team daily.  
Support preparation of stock and equipment.  
Prioritize tasks and manage progress.  
Excluded from store refresh or presentation, backfill GM, and returns tasks.  
Equipment includes P2T, PDT, gloves, toolbelt.

## Shop Keeping as Part of Store Operations
Relies on accurate filling, standardized presentation, and completed returns.  
Impacts effective filling, customer shopping experience, product return speed, and layout spotting.  
Supports prioritization of shop keeping efforts.

## Refresh Role & Accountabilities
Collect a cage, attend the huddle, be assigned an area.  
Start with safety and misplaced items.  
Sort items by world as placed on cages.  
Return items to their display when encountered.  
Provide targets for presentation on subsequent laps.  
Help with customer assistance as needed.

## Refresh Preparation
- Collect a refresh cage.  
- Attend team huddle.  
- Get assigned store area.  
- Prioritize safety and misplaced items.  
- Sort collected items.  
- Restock display areas as they are visited.  
- Provide collected items from others or empty packets to the refresh manager.  
- Assist customers when needed.

## Refresh Monitoring
Perform regular check-ins: 30-minute pace and quality checks, hourly return area checks.  
Use a schedule grid to plan shifts, assign activities, and track progress.  
Team members start and end shifts with clear task awareness and validation.

## Refreshing Principles
Effective from end of sequence until presentation begins.  
Address safety, misplaced products, rubbish, and priority areas.  
Plan and prepare by estimating team size and assigning tasks.  
Manage pace and quality through scheduled check-ins.  
Team members perform initial 30-minute safety and pick-up laps, then continue with priority areas.  
Sort products into cages, redistribute as needed.  
End of shift involves returning equipment and team huddles.

## Refresh Equipment
Push to talk (P2T), PDT, cage, tool belt, gloves.

## Refresh Exclusions
Does not include detailed presentation, VM, sizing, returns, gap fixing, or layout fixes.

## Returns Role & Accountabilities
Attend huddles.  
Collect sorted returns cage or rail.  
Assess volume, sortation, and plan route.  
Return items by category.  
Update the refresh manager on progress.  
Provide customer assistance as required.  
Collect new cages or rails when done.

## Returns Sortation (Example: Apparel and Central Checkouts)
Micro-sort categories using discs on rails to improve efficiency.  
Full rails are moved to BOH for processing or further sorting.  
Effective sortation reduces processing time by approximately 25%.  
Effectively sort apparel into totes, and redistribute to fitting rooms if needed.  
At checkouts, use categories like Home, Kids, Clothing, with appropriate containment and discipline.

## Returns on Operation
Depend on effective sortation, store standards maintained by shop keeping and refresh teams, and processed returns from those teams.  
Impacts processing time and product availability.

## Returns Principles
Align with refresh and presentation.  
Use dedicated resources and areas for returns.  
Micro-sort returns by category into cages, rails, or totes.  
Manage volume and sortation through shift planning.  
Move products in a single direction, put products back as encountered.  
Sort miscellaneous and end-of-range items as needed.  
Use equipment including P2T, cages, gloves, PDT, tool belt.  
Exclude RLO, rubbish, refresh, and presentation from returns scope.

## Store Presentation Role & Accountabilities
Attend huddles, implement standards, and monitor progress.  
Use a cage to facilitate track and control.  
Complete regular check-ins (every ~20 minutes).  
Lead and coach the team to improve pace and quality.  
Coordinate with team for returns and other processes.

Ensure shifts are planned based on predictive data.  
Generate detailed weekly and store-specific rostering schedules.

## Store Labour Hours Summary & Rostering
Provides detailed hours forecasted for services, sales floor, back of house, admin, and conditional hours.  
Includes weekly summary of key tasks and team sizes.  
Supports effective staffing and resource management per store volume and activity patterns.
