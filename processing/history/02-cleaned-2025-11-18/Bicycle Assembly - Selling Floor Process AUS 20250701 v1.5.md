# Bicycle Assembly Process

## Selling Area

Store Area: Leisure Manager

Work is performed prior to the visit from the Hunter Service representative, during and after the visit, when selling an assembled bicycle to a customer, and in maintaining standards of the bicycle unit.

### Activity Steps

#### Prior to visit

Perform a visual check of the bicycle unit to determine the number of bicycle checklists requiring replacement.

Ensure appropriate space is available for the assembly of bicycles, not on the selling area.

Make sure bicycles requiring assembly and/or repair are ready and accessible for the Hunter Service representative.

Ensure the designated work area is safe and free from obstructions.

#### During visit

Clearly communicate the work required to the Hunter Service representative, whether it is repair work or assembly of bicycles, and the number of assembly checklists that need replacing as per the visual check.

Sign work invoices promptly for the completion of agreed works, ensuring all bicycles on the display unit have a completed Hunter Service Assembly Checklist attached by cable ties to the top tube or handlebar of the bicycle.

Attach the manufacturer warranty and instruction booklet to the bicycle seat post of assembled bicycles.

Discuss and agree on the following week's assembly and/or repair requirements, including any promotional or lay-by potential requirements.

Discuss the need for additional visits within the same week if required due to higher than normal sales expectations.

### Ongoing Post Visit (maintaining bicycle unit standards)

Rectify missing Point of Sale material from the bicycle display unit as discussed with the Hunter Service representative.

Install Point of Sale material as required, highlighting to the customer:
- The assembly charge of $16.50.
- That bicycles are sold in cartons, and display models are not for sale; bicycles are to remain in the unit.

Attach the Hunter Service Assembly Checklist, completed by an authorized Hunter Service representative, to the top tube or handlebar of all display bicycles.

Attach the manufacturer warranty and instruction booklet to the bicycle seat post of all assembled bicycles.

### When selling a display or sample bicycle

The customer is not charged the bicycle assembly fee when:
- The display model is a clearance item.
- The display model is the last stock on hand of that model.

Ensure that a K412 Soiled & Damaged Stock Sticker (keycode 36411863) is displayed on any previously returned bicycle being sold, including a completed Hunter Service Assembly Checklist, manufacturer warranty, and instruction booklet.

## Charging Requirements

The customer is charged the $16.50 assembly fee when the customer:
- Specifically requests to purchase a display model.
- Requests to have a model assembled by the Hunter Service representative.

The customer is not charged for any bicycle repair covered by the warranty conditions.

## Contacts

Contact Hunter Service (Service Manager) via the 1800 632 792 number for urgent assembly or repair needs or issues requiring resolution.

### When a call is answered by Hunter Service

The caller should introduce themselves by name, provide store number and store name, and clearly state the issue or question in a professional and polite manner.

Provide any requested additional information to assist in resolving the issue or query.

### When leaving a message

The caller should introduce themselves by name, provide store number and store name, and clearly state the issue or question professionally and politely.

## Activity Measurement

Each assembled bicycle will have a Hunter Service Assembly Checklist attached.

Assembly and repair of all bicycle brands will be carried out by the authorized Hunter Service representative.

The bicycle display unit must be maintained to the required standard of presentation.

All bicycles sold will conform to the Hunter Service Assembly Checklist criteria.
